# Description
Repair a damaged nature or city photo while preserving the scene, composition, and era-appropriate details exactly. Use when you have an aged or scanned landscape/cityscape with scratches, tears, dust, fading, stains, or noise.

# Prompt
Edit the provided photograph. Treat it as the source and keep it the same photograph — do not regenerate, reinterpret, replace, or re-stage the scene, and do not invent new buildings, terrain, or sky. Apply only the damage-repair edits described below as a careful, non-destructive restoration, and keep everything else pixel-faithful to the source image. This is a repair job, not a reinterpretation or a cinematic restyle.

Tasks:
- remove scratches, scuff lines, tears, fold and crease lines
- remove dust specks, dirt, spots, water stains, mold and mildew marks, foxing, surface blemishes
- reduce grain and noise to a clean but natural level
- correct fading, color shifts, and discoloration so the image looks balanced again
- recover lost contrast and detail in shadows and highlights
- restore detail in foliage, trees, grass, water, stone and brick, building facades, windows, roads, and sky
- reconstruct small missing or destroyed areas plausibly, matching the surrounding architecture, terrain, texture, and lighting direction
- keep all era-appropriate details — buildings, signage, vehicles, vegetation, period look — exactly as in the source
- keep the composition, framing, perspective, and all content exactly as in the source

Output style: clean, undamaged, naturally lit photograph; believable materials, depth, and tones; faithful to the original era, time of day, and weather; photorealistic.

Negative constraints: do not regenerate or restage the scene; do not change the composition, framing, or perspective; do not modernize the scene; do not change the time of day, weather, or mood; do not add, remove, or move any buildings, objects, vehicles, or elements; do not restyle or apply a cinematic grade; no stylization; no over-sharpening; no AI-looking textures; no warped geometry; keep everything else unchanged.
