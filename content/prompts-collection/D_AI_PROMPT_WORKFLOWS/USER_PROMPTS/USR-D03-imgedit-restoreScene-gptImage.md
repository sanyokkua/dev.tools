# Description
Repair physical damage on an old or damaged landscape or cityscape photo while preserving the exact scene, composition, and era-appropriate details. Use this when you have an aged or scanned nature or city photo with scratches, dust, fading, stains, creases, or noise and you want a clean, natural restoration that keeps the original place and period intact.

# Prompt
This is an old, damaged photograph of an outdoor scene — a landscape or a city view — that has been scanned. Carefully restore it to look like a clean, well-preserved photograph. Repair all physical damage: remove scratches, tears, creases, fold lines, dust, spots, stains, water marks, mold, and film grain or scan noise; fix faded, yellowed, or discolored areas and reconstruct any missing or torn regions by realistically extending the surrounding detail. Recover lost fine detail in foliage, trees, grass, rock, water, buildings, brickwork, windows, signage, roads, and the sky so textures look sharp and natural. Rebalance contrast, exposure, and tonal range for an even, lifelike result with realistic shadows, highlights, and depth. Keep the scene exactly as it is — same composition, framing, perspective, horizon, structures, objects, and any era-appropriate details such as vintage vehicles, clothing of people, period architecture, or signage. Do not modernize, add, remove, or rearrange anything, and do not crop or restyle the image. Output a photorealistic, modern-quality photograph at the same aspect ratio as the uploaded image, with natural, believable colors.
