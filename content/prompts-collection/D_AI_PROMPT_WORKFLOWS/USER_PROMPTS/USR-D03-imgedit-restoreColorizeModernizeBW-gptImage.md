# Description
Revive an old black-and-white photo in one pass — repair damage, add natural color, and bring it to modern flagship-camera quality, keeping the scene and people exactly the same. Use this when you have an old, damaged, faded, or black-and-white photograph and want a single prompt that restores, colorizes, and modernizes it at once.

# Prompt
I'm uploading an old, damaged black-and-white (or faded/sepia) photograph, and I'd like you to revive it in a single pass so it looks like the exact same scene photographed today on a high-end modern camera.

Please do three things at once. First, restore it: carefully remove the scratches, tears, creases, fold lines, dust, spots, stains, grain, and noise, reconstruct any missing or worn detail, and reverse the fading, haze, and yellowing. Second, colorize it: add natural, realistic, era-appropriate color across the whole image — believable skin tones, natural hair and eye color, and coherent colors for clothing, fabrics, objects, background, plants, and sky — kept grounded and plausible for the time period so it still feels true to the original era. Third, modernize the technical quality: clean sharpness, recovered fine detail, accurate white balance, realistic lighting, wide dynamic range with subtle HDR-like depth, and faithful material realism in skin, fabric, metal, wood, glass, and foliage.

Make sure real texture stays intact and visible — skin pores, fine facial detail, and fabric weave — with no plastic smoothing, no waxy or AI-looking skin, no overprocessing.

Preserve everything real about the original. Keep the faces exactly as they appear in the uploaded image — same eyes, nose, mouth, and face shape. Keep the same people, identities, expressions, ages, poses, body proportions, hairstyles, and clothing design. Keep the same objects, background, scene layout, composition, camera angle, and framing. Don't add, remove, move, or restyle anyone or anything — only repair the damage, add the color, and lift the quality.

Output a clean, photorealistic image in the same aspect ratio as the original so nothing is cropped, looking like a sharp modern professional camera shot.
