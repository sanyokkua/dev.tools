# Description
Transform a cartoon or anime image into a photorealistic real photograph while preserving pose, outfit, and character identity. Use when you have an illustrated or anime image and want a believable real-photo version of it.

# Prompt
Edit the provided illustration by converting it into a photorealistic real photograph, as if the character or scene were captured by a camera. Keep the same character or subjects, their pose, facial expression, hairstyle, outfit, accessories, color scheme, and the overall composition and framing as the source — the result must clearly be the same character brought to life realistically. Only change the rendering medium from drawn to photographic, as described below; do not re-stage the scene, change who the character is, or rearrange the layout.

Tasks:
- re-render with real-world physics and materials throughout
- give lifelike skin with natural texture, pores, and subtle tones
- render real hair with individual strands and natural shine
- render fabric with believable weave, folds, and weight
- render realistic metal, leather, plastic, glass, and other materials
- use natural, physically plausible lighting with soft shadows, accurate highlights, and proper depth
- place the subject in a coherent, realistic version of the original setting
- translate stylized proportions into natural, believable human anatomy while keeping the character's identity and recognizable look intact
- keep the same pose, outfit, color scheme, expression, and composition as the source

Output style: convincing photorealistic photograph; realistic skin, hair, and fabric; natural physically plausible lighting; realistic optics and depth; detailed but natural.

Negative constraints: do not keep a cartoon look or any stylization; do not leave cel shading, flat colors, or line art; do not change who the character is; do not change the pose, expression, outfit, accessories, or color scheme; do not rearrange the scene or change the composition; no plastic or waxy skin; no AI-looking skin; no warped anatomy; no extra fingers; no distorted eyes; keep the character's identity, pose, and layout the same while the medium becomes a real photograph.
