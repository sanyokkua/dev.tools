# Description
Raise the quality of a nature or city photo — clarity, detail, dynamic range, natural color — without changing the scene or composition. Use when you have an undamaged but soft, hazy, flat, or low-quality landscape/cityscape that just needs to look better.

# Prompt
Edit the provided photograph. Treat it as the source and keep it the same photograph — do not regenerate, reinterpret, replace, or re-stage the scene, and do not invent new buildings, terrain, or sky. Assume there is no physical damage to repair; this is purely a non-destructive quality upgrade. Apply only the quality improvements described below and keep everything else pixel-faithful to the source image.

Tasks:
- remove blur, JPEG and compression artifacts, sensor noise, color noise, chromatic aberration
- dehaze the image to restore clarity and visibility into the distance
- increase real sharpness and fine detail in foliage, trees, grass, water, stone, brick, building facades, windows, railings, roads, and surface textures
- recover dynamic range so the sky, shadows, and highlights all hold detail
- create clean, subtle HDR-like tonal depth while remaining photorealistic
- improve white balance and color accuracy so greens, blues, skies, and materials look natural and true
- preserve good depth separation and natural local contrast
- keep the scene, composition, framing, perspective, and every element exactly as in the source

Output style: ultra-clean modern flagship-camera quality; realistic optics; natural colors; subtle HDR; high clarity; fully photorealistic; detailed but natural.

Negative constraints: do not regenerate or restage the scene; do not change the composition, framing, or perspective; do not change the time of day, weather, or mood; do not add, remove, or move any buildings, objects, vehicles, or elements; do not restyle or apply a cinematic grade; no oversaturation; no overprocessing; no excessive sharpening; no stylization; no AI-looking textures; no warped geometry; keep everything else unchanged.
