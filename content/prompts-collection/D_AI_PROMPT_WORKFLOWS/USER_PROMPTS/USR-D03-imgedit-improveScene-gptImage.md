# Description
Raise the technical quality of an undamaged landscape or cityscape photo — clarity, detail, dynamic range, and color — while keeping the scene and composition exactly the same. Use this when you have an intact but low-quality nature or city photo (soft, noisy, compressed, hazy, flat, or dull) and you want a crisp, high-quality version of the same shot.

# Prompt
This is a real, undamaged outdoor photo — a landscape or city scene, likely a casual smartphone or compressed image — that simply needs higher quality. Do not treat this as damaged; just improve the image quality. Remove blur, focus softness, JPEG compression artifacts, sensor noise, grain, atmospheric haze, and color fringing or chromatic aberration; dehaze distant areas for clearer visibility. Increase real clarity, sharpness, and micro-contrast on architecture, brickwork, windows, signage, roads, foliage, trees, grass, water, rock, and clouds so textures look detailed and natural. Recover detail and dynamic range in shadows and bright skies, and improve lighting realism, white balance, and color accuracy for natural, believable tones with good depth. Keep the scene exactly as it is — same composition, framing, perspective, horizon, structures, objects, people, and layout — and do not add, remove, move, recolor, or restyle anything. Output a photorealistic, high-resolution photograph at the same aspect ratio as the uploaded image, with natural colors and clean, professional-camera clarity. Avoid an over-saturated or over-processed HDR look.
