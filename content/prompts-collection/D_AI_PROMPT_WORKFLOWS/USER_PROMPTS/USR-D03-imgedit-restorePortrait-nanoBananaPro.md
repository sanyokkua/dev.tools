# Description
Repair a damaged old portrait while keeping the people's faces, identity, expression, and composition exactly the same. Use when you have a scanned or aged portrait photo with scratches, tears, creases, dust, fading, stains, or heavy grain.

# Prompt
Edit the provided photograph. Treat it as the source and keep it the same photograph — do not regenerate, reinterpret, replace, or re-stage the scene, and do not invent new people or a new setting. Apply only the damage-repair edits described below as a careful, non-destructive restoration, and keep everything else pixel-faithful to the source image. This is a repair job, not a makeover or a quality restyle.

Tasks:
- remove scratches, scuff lines, tears, rips, fold and crease lines
- remove dust specks, dirt, spots, water stains, mildew and mold marks, foxing, surface blemishes
- reduce film grain and noise to a clean but natural level
- correct fading and discoloration so tones look balanced again; fix uneven exposure and color shifts
- recover lost contrast and detail in shadows and highlights
- reconstruct small missing or destroyed areas of face, hair, skin, eyes, and clothing plausibly, matching the surrounding texture, lighting direction, and grain
- restore believable skin texture, hair detail, and fabric detail where damage erased them
- keep all people, faces, objects, positions, and scene layout exactly as in the source

Output style: clean, undamaged, naturally lit photograph; believable skin texture and fabric detail; realistic, balanced tones; photorealistic; faithful to the original era and lighting.

Negative constraints: do not regenerate or restage the scene; do not change identity; do not change facial structure or features; do not change expression; do not change hairstyle, clothing, pose, or composition; do not beautify, slim, smooth, or retouch anyone; do not restyle; do not change the lighting mood, background, or era of clothing and setting; do not add or remove people or objects; no AI-looking or waxy skin; no over-smoothing; no over-sharpening; no stylization; no warped anatomy; no extra fingers; no distorted eyes; keep everything else unchanged.
